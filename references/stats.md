## What drives the price up (use when explaining price differences)

- Private room vs. shared: typically adds $150–$400/week
- Luxury destination (Tuscany, Tulum beachfront): adds $500–$1,500/week vs. comparable inland retreat
- Small group (under 12): typically 20–35% premium over large-group retreats
- Internationally recognised teacher: adds $200–$600/week
- Off-peak vs. peak season: 15–30% discount possible in shoulder months

## Industry benchmarks

- Global wellness tourism market: $919 billion (2023), projected $1.4 trillion by 2027 (Global Wellness Institute)
- Average yoga retreat length: 5–7 days (most popular format globally)
- Typical refund policy window: 30–60 days before start date for full or partial refund; always confirm individually
- Typical group size: 8–20 participants (sweet spot for community without losing individual attention)
- Solo travellers account for approximately 60–65% of retreat bookings (BookRetreats data, 2024)
- Women account for approximately 72% of retreat attendees globally

## What "value" means on this site

We define value as: *quality of teaching + quality of accommodation + quality of programming, relative to price paid.* A $400 retreat in Rishikesh with outstanding teachers outranks a $2,000 retreat in Bali with mediocre ones. Price alone is never the rating criterion.

## Red flag stats (use in "how to choose" content)

- Retreats with fewer than 5 verifiable independent reviews: approach with caution
- Deposit requirements above 50% of total price before a 30-day window: atypical, worth questioning
- Teacher-to-participant ratio worse than 1:20 for yoga instruction: likely to affect quality
- Retreats that don't disclose teacher credentials publicly: a consistent red flag across Sarah's experience
